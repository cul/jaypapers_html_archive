"Thinks it hard if "the commission under the law respecting people of suspicious or equivocal character against Mr. Peter Van Schaack" should subject him to penalties on his return to New York, but not willing to say whether this would be the case in a "rigid construction of the law", of which Jay is a better judge than he. Mr. Fletcher Mathews in same situation; he remains in the country without a problem so presumes he is not considered subject to the penalty. Commission determined both men should be sent within British lines, but instead were detained by his [Clinton∆s] order. Only difference in their cases: Mr. Mathews stayed in the country while Mr. Van Schaick, on Êparole∆, permitted to go to England for an operation on his eye. As he behaved with honor and humanity while there, Clinton favorable to him.
