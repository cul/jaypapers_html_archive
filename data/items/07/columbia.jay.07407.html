Translation of passages from an unknown book which "agrees [with] Triglantius in his Historia Ecclesiastica," dealing with the founding of the Dutch Church in England in 1550 under Edward VI, the establishment of its government, catechisms and books of psalms and hymns written for its use, and doctrinal and controversial writings. It notes that it was "remarkable that no English or Scotch Presbyterians were at the Synod of Dort, nor does it appear from the Acts that they were as much as asked", because the convention of the Synod was "in great measure owing to the earnest Sollicatations of King James I." Another passage notes that different synods in the United Provinces were invited to join the clergymen assembled in the Jerusalem Chamber at Westminster in 1640, but that the invitations were received "with the Contempt they deserved & were answered cum silentio." In Rudolphus Ritzema's hand.
