Last letter sent by Major Franks; a copy of that letter will be sent by Mr. Stephen Codman of Boston, who is passing through Madrid on his way to Cadiz. Still no change in the delay tactics practiced by the Spanish minister. Has learned from Franklin that despite all their efforts, they will have to default on some of the bills drawn on Jay∆s name. Is suffering from rheumatism; as soon as well promises to write a longer and more detailed letter. Cannot read Livingston∆s letter of December 13 without a key and thinks Secretary Thomson∆s code has been read and is therefore too risky to use. Believes should persevere in their efforts in Spain despite the obstacles and that (the threat of) Jay∆s recall might perhaps be used in hopes of getting a final answer from the Spanish court. Thinks it a good idea to consult with Franklin and Adams and asks Congress about propriety of going to France or Holland for that purpose. Although sees that the appearance of Êbeing well∆ in Spain is important to maintain, still believes that it would be better to push the Spanish for a definitive answer than continue to wait. Does not think the Spanish will break with the Americans, but the French ambassador is against the idea.
