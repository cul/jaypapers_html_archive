Is grateful for the honor of having been elected president of the society. It can be presumed that the fallen state of man and the institution of sacrifices relating to his redemption have been known to every antediluvian generation. After the division of men into nations, true religion was gradually supplanted by fables and tribal rites. Early provision was made for the preservation of truth through Abraham, whose nation became a theocracy from which the Redeemer would originate. Records are set forth in the Bible which prepare us for everlasting life. The Gospel began to triumph when it was published; however it has met with opposition in the conversion of all gentiles. This will be overcome by the instrumentality of Christian nations in future. The late war in Europe resulted in calamities, yet along with a concern for immediate problems, exertions of a different kind are seen. A Bible Society was formed in England and its example is followed by other countries. For many generations the slave trade was countenanced by professing Christians; in Britain and America this obstacle has been removed and other nations are following the example. Christian nations, formerly indifferent, have come to feel concern for the salvation of heathen souls as in India. These events can only be attributed to "the Wisdom that cometh from above." We must conclude that the Redeemer is preparing to take possession of his heritage. We have abundant reason to rejoice in the blessings of our endeavors and must persevere in distributing the scriptures following the example of the Apostles. Admonishes opponents with a quotation to "refrain from these men," for if their "work be of men, it will come to naught--but if it be of God, ye cannot overthrow it."
