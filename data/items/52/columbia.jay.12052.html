Necker's return has not restored the expected tranquillity. There has been some violence in Paris, where a few rioters were killed by the city militia. Baron de Besenval and the Duke de la Vauguyon, who were trying to flee, were captured and imprisoned. Luzerne continues in his position, and others like the Archbishop of Bordeaux, De la Tour de Pin, and the Prince of Beauvau have been named to the ministry. The National Assembly is at work on a declaration of rights. Jefferson fears that the number of those called by Necker to the meeting, one thousand two hundred persons, will make the formulation of the law difficult. The desire of the Parisians to form a municipal government independent of the authority of the nation at large may be a danger to the unity of the country.
