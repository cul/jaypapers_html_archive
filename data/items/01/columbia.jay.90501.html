ALS. Written by Jay as Secretary for Foreign Affairs under the Confederation government to Price, a dissenting minister and pamphleteer in Britain who supported American independence. Apologizes to Price for being tardy in responding and thanks him for sending copies of his pamphlet (a reference to Price's pamphlet "Observations on the importance of the American Revolution, and the means of making it a benefit to the world." which Price had sent Jay in 1784, and which among other themes argued for elimination of slavery in America). Jay says "I am not without Hopes that Time and Experience will yet bring our political system to that Degree of Perfection, which may reasonably be expected in human Institutions," adding "popular Governments must be influenced by popular opinion, and popular opinion must be created not commanded," a process that can "proceed but slowly." Jay notes that Price's pamphet has been reprinted, read, and admired in America and that Price's character, integrity, and abilities are well respected there.
