Jefferson asks that John Jay decipher the partly coded letter himself, and, for the time being, that he communicate its contents to no one but the President. Jefferson has told Montmorin that the Count de Moustier's conduct and his harshness in reminding Americans of the debt owed to France might influence unfavorably the friendship of the two countries, and has asked that he be recalled. Although there is no other vacancy at present, Montmorin might give Moustier a leave of absence, and send Colonel Ternant to America as Charge d'Affaires. Moustier's leave will become in effect a recall, and Ternant, if Washington approves of him, will become Minister. Duplicate, containing on back of third page coded messages dated 14 March and 11 May 1789, evidently enclosed in Jefferson to John Jay, 11 May 1789.
