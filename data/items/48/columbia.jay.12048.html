The Archbishop of Paris and other members of the clergy and nobility have joined the third estate. The deputation from Santo Domingo was received. The guards, even those belonging to the King, began to assemble and to declare that they would guard the King but would not kill their fellow citizens. The King wrote letters to the President of the Clergy and the President of the Nobility asking them to join the commons, and this was done. As soon as this became known, the people at Versailles rejoiced and acclaimed the King, the Queen, and the Dauphin. It remains to be seen whether the third estate will leave to the nobility anything but their titles. Necker will probably manage to remain in office by siding with the third estate.
