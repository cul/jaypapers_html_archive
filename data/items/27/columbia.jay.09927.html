Coded letter. Sent four copies by separate ships. Major Franks received Jay∆s letter enumerating slights by Spanish, which, when communicated to Congress, "excited their warmest resentment". Eventually came to a (somewhat tempered) resolution, enclosed. Do not want to jeopardize commerce; particularly important to Spain because lacking own manufacture. Unsure of Floridablanca∆s views on commercial relations with Spanish colonies. Strongly wishes to avoid mutual guarantee of each other∆s territory; might be harmful to future interest and conscience, and after the war of little use to the United States in any case. Must prevent guarantee from extending to Spanish conquest on the Mississippi. Reports that Savannah evacuated but no official account. Continental army still on the Hudson river, approximately 12,000 men. French army of approximately 5,000 marching to same post. Marquis de Vaudreuil and 13 ships of 1400 men at bases will remain to protect trading ships and then to Boston to refit ships. Southern armies same. About to attend debate in Congress on what instructions to give ministers in Europe for making peace. Will pay Jay and other ministers through Franklin; another quarter salary by this post.
