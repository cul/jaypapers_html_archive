Chancery case involving James Duane's executors as trustees for Peter DuBois's creditors. Asks Peter to open packet with subscription papers for Memoirs of General Henry Lee. Attaches copy of his letter to the general's brother, Richard Bland Lee, concerning the project. Wants to promote the subscription as the Lees "have been constantly among my friends" and because general's treatment at Baltimore "should excite the Feelings of the Federalists." Suggests groups in New York that could help. Hopes to promote sales "not only from political motives, but because the Proceeds must be interesting to the General & his family." Jay's note to Richard Bland Lee of September 12 follows, detailing Jay's interest in the forthcoming memoir and his indignation at "the Outrages committed at Baltimore."
