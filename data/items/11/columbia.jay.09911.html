The deponent said that 15 or 16 years ago he was deputy sheriff for Orange County, and before that time he had served writs on three men who lived 6 or 7 miles southwest of Richard Gardner's house. About 12 or 14 years before that there had been three lines run between New York and New Jersey: Clinton's line, another run in 1719, and a third run by a certain Wells. This last line had not been recorded in New York, but Anthony Westbrook had indicated to the deponent a certain tree southwest of his house as a boundary, and had told him that the line run by Wells was about a rod south of it. In answer to cross interrogation, Keeler said that about 12 years before, he had gone to serve a writ in Minisink with a party of men. A number of their party were taken prisoner, and forced to appear at the Court of Common Pleas in New Jersey, where they were indicted for inciting a riot. Signed, Ebenezer Keeler. In Jay's hand.
